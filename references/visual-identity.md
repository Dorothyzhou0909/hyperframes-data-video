# Visual Identity

Create or update `DESIGN.md` before authoring a composition.

## Brand Discovery

Use these inputs in priority order:

1. User-provided brand guide, deck, website, logo, or screenshot.
2. Official annual report, investor deck, product site, or press page.
3. Industry context and the content tone.
4. A minimal custom identity if none exists.

## DESIGN.md Contents

Include:

- Style prompt: one paragraph describing mood and context.
- Colors: roles, not just hex values.
- Typography: display voice, data voice, body voice.
- Motion: transition language, chart behavior, lock/accent behavior.
- What not to do: 3-6 concrete anti-patterns.

## Color Guidance

- Do not infer the whole video from one logo color. A red logo does not automatically mean a black-red market video.
- Reserve brand accent colors for logo, final values, locks, and meaningful emphasis.
- Use background colors from the brand's broader world: product surfaces, annual report covers, website hero sections, or industry imagery.
- Avoid default red/green up/down unless requested. Use direction, contraction, negative signs, and labels instead.
- On dark canvases, keep body text high-contrast and make small labels at least 16px in rendered 1080p video.

## Corporate Data Tone

Prefer restrained confidence:

- Clean horizon lines, controlled glows, thin rules, proportional bands, and precise typography.
- No confetti, playful bounce, exaggerated trading graphics, or decorative logo patterns unless the brand calls for them.
- For public companies, make the tone analytical rather than promotional unless the user explicitly asks for an ad.
